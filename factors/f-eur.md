# F_EUR: European Stress Factor

## Overview

The European Stress factor represents political, economic, and social pressure within Europe—EU cohesion, fiscal sustainability, migration dynamics, and political stability. When F_EUR has a high realization, European crises become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- EU political cohesion and fragmentation risk
- Eurozone fiscal sustainability
- Migration pressure and politics
- Rise of nationalist/populist movements
- Energy security (post-Russia dependence)
- Demographic pressure (aging, low fertility)

## Events That Load Heavily on F_EUR

| Event | Loading | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| EU Fragmentation | 0.85 | This IS the factor manifesting |
| Eurozone Sovereign Crisis | 0.75 | Fiscal dimension of European stress |
| European Migration Crisis | 0.70 | Social/political dimension |
| [[amoc-weakening]] | 0.15 | Europe is affected, but not cause |
| Turkey Political Crisis | 0.35 | EU relationship, migration gateway |

## Geographic Scope

F_EUR affects:
- EU member states (especially fiscally stressed Southern and Eastern Europe)
- UK (post-Brexit but still economically linked)
- EU candidate countries (Turkey, Balkans)
- Maghreb (migration transit, EU economic ties)

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_FIN**: Strong relationship. European fiscal crises have financial dimensions.
- **F_MENA**: Moderate relationship. MENA instability drives migration pressure.
- **F_CLIM**: Moderate relationship via Southern European climate stress.
- **F_GPT**: Moderate relationship via NATO cohesion, Russia policy.

## Calibration Notes

European stress is currently moderate (post-COVID recovery, Ukraine war adaptation, but populist pressures and demographic challenges persistent).
