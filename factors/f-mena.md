# F_MENA: Middle East / North Africa Instability Factor

## Overview

The MENA Instability factor represents regional stress across the Middle East and North Africa—state fragility, conflicts, sectarian tensions, and resource pressures. When F_MENA has a high realization, regional crises become more likely across the region.

## What This Factor Captures

- State fragility and failure risk
- Sectarian and ethnic tensions
- Oil/energy market dynamics
- Water stress (Nile, Tigris-Euphrates)
- Regional conflict and intervention
- Regime stability in key states
- Israel-Palestine dynamics

## Geographic Scope

F_MENA affects:
- Gulf states (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Iran, Iraq)
- Levant (Syria, Lebanon, Jordan, Israel/Palestine)
- Egypt and Nile basin
- Maghreb (Libya, Tunisia, Algeria, Morocco)
- Yemen, Horn of Africa (peripheral)

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_FOOD**: Strong relationship. MENA is heavily food-import dependent.
- **F_CLIM**: Strong relationship. Water stress is severe and worsening.
- **F_EUR**: Moderate relationship via migration and energy.
- **F_GPT**: Moderate relationship. Great powers contest influence.
- **F_FIN**: Moderate relationship. Oil prices affect fiscal sustainability.

## Calibration Notes

MENA instability is currently elevated (Gaza conflict, Yemen, Syrian aftermath, Iranian tensions). Baseline probabilities reflect this starting condition.
