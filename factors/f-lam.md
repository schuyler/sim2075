# F_LAM: Latin American Stress Factor

## Overview

The Latin American Stress factor represents regional pressure across Latin America—economic volatility, political instability, commodity dependence, and governance challenges. When F_LAM has a high realization, Latin American crises become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- Economic/fiscal crises (chronic in several countries)
- Political instability and populism
- Commodity export dependence
- Venezuelan spillover and migration
- Drug trafficking and organized crime
- Democratic backsliding
- Amazon deforestation politics

## Events That Load Heavily on F_LAM

| Event | Loading | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Brazilian Political Crisis | 0.75 | Largest regional economy |
| Argentine Economic Collapse | 0.80 | Chronic crisis case |
| Venezuelan Further Collapse | 0.70 | Ongoing regional crisis |
| Central American Humanitarian Crisis | 0.65 | Migration pressure |
| Amazon Tipping Point | 0.30 | Brazilian policy driver |
| Mexican Instability | 0.55 | Second-largest economy |

## Geographic Scope

F_LAM affects:
- Brazil (largest economy, Amazon custodian)
- Mexico (US neighbor, NAFTA integration)
- Argentina (chronic crisis case)
- Colombia, Peru, Chile (Andean economies)
- Central America (migration source)
- Venezuela (ongoing collapse, regional spillover)
- Caribbean (small island states, remittance dependent)

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_FIN**: Strong relationship. Latin American crises often have financial triggers.
- **F_CLIM**: Moderate relationship via Amazon and agricultural exposure.
- **F_FOOD**: Moderate relationship. Region is net food exporter but vulnerable.
- **F_GPT**: Weak-moderate relationship. US sphere but China increasing presence.
- **F_EUR, F_MENA, F_SAS, F_EAS**: Weak relationship. Latin America is relatively isolated.

## Migration Dimension

Central American stress creates migration pressure on US southern border. Venezuelan collapse has created largest displacement crisis in Western Hemisphere (~7+ million).

## Calibration Notes

Latin American stress is moderate. Argentina in chronic crisis, Venezuela collapsed, but Brazil and Mexico relatively stable. Commodity prices currently supportive.
