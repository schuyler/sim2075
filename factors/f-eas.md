# F_EAS: East Asian Tension Factor

## Overview

The East Asian Tension factor represents regional stress in East Asia—Taiwan Strait dynamics, Korean peninsula risk, South China Sea disputes, and China's regional assertiveness. When F_EAS has a high realization, East Asian conflicts become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- Taiwan Strait tension and invasion risk
- Korean peninsula stability
- South China Sea territorial disputes
- US alliance commitments (Japan, Korea, Philippines)
- China-Japan relations
- North Korean provocations and instability

## Events That Load Heavily on F_EAS

| Event | Loading | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Taiwan Conflict | 0.80 | Core regional event |
| Korean Peninsula Crisis | 0.75 | Core regional event |
| South China Sea Conflict | 0.70 | Core regional event |
| North Korea Collapse | 0.60 | Regional instability |
| Japan Political Transformation | 0.45 | Regional power shift |
| Semiconductor Supply Collapse | 0.50 | Taiwan concentration |

## Geographic Scope

F_EAS affects:
- China (1.4 billion people, second-largest economy)
- Japan (third-largest economy)
- South Korea (major economy, divided peninsula)
- Taiwan (semiconductor concentration)
- North Korea (nuclear wildcard)
- ASEAN (peripheral but affected)

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_GPT**: Very strong relationship. East Asia is primary arena of US-China competition.
- **F_FIN**: Moderate relationship. Regional conflict would devastate global supply chains.
- **F_TECH**: Moderate relationship. Semiconductor concentration in Taiwan.
- **F_CLIM**: Weak relationship. Climate not primary driver of East Asian tensions.

## Critical Infrastructure Concentration

Taiwan produces ~90% of advanced semiconductors. F_EAS events that affect Taiwan have global supply chain implications far exceeding the island's size.

## Calibration Notes

East Asian tension is elevated and rising. US-China competition intensifying, Taiwan military pressure increasing, North Korea expanding nuclear/missile capabilities.
