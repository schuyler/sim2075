# F_TECH: Technology Disruption Factor

## Overview

The Technology Disruption factor represents the pace and disruptiveness of technological change—particularly AI, cyber capabilities, and critical infrastructure vulnerability. When F_TECH has a high realization, technology-driven discontinuities become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- AI capability advancement rate
- Labor market disruption from automation
- Cybersecurity vulnerabilities
- Critical infrastructure exposure
- Technology governance gaps
- Dual-use technology proliferation

## Events That Load Heavily on F_TECH

| Event | Loading | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| AI Disruption (Severe) | 0.85 | This IS the factor manifesting |
| Major Cyber Infrastructure Attack | 0.70 | Cyber capability/vulnerability |
| Semiconductor Supply Collapse | 0.40 | Tech concentration risk |
| Renewable Energy Breakthrough | 0.50 | Positive technology discontinuity |

## Correlation Implications

F_TECH is relatively independent of other factors. Technology disruptions can occur regardless of climate, financial, or geopolitical conditions.

However, technology events can interact with other factors:
- Cyber attacks during geopolitical tension (F_GPT correlation)
- AI disruption affecting labor markets and political stability

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_GPT**: Moderate relationship. Tech competition is part of great power rivalry.
- **F_FIN**: Weak-moderate relationship. Tech disruption affects economic structure.
- **Most other factors**: Weak relationship. Technology operates somewhat independently.

## Calibration Challenges

Technology disruption is the hardest category to calibrate:
- No historical base rates for transformative AI
- Rapid capability change makes extrapolation unreliable
- Expert forecasts vary enormously

Probability estimates for F_TECH events have the lowest confidence of any category.
