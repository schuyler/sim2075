# F_SAS: South Asian Stress Factor

## Overview

The South Asian Stress factor represents regional pressure across the Indian subcontinent—water stress, India-Pakistan tensions, climate vulnerability, and state fragility. When F_SAS has a high realization, South Asian crises become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- India-Pakistan rivalry and nuclear risk
- Water stress (Indus, Ganges-Brahmaputra)
- Glacial retreat and river flow changes
- Bangladesh climate/sea level exposure
- State fragility (Pakistan, Bangladesh)
- Hindu-Muslim communal tensions
- Economic development pressures

## Geographic Scope

F_SAS affects:
- India (1.4 billion people)
- Pakistan (240 million)
- Bangladesh (175 million)
- Nepal, Sri Lanka, Afghanistan (peripheral)

The region contains ~25% of humanity. Regional instability has global implications.

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_CLIM**: Very strong relationship. South Asia is extremely climate-vulnerable.
- **F_FOOD**: Strong relationship. Agricultural dependence, water-food nexus.
- **F_GPT**: Moderate relationship. Great power competition for influence.
- **F_HLTH**: Moderate relationship. Health system capacity varies.

## Nuclear Dimension

South Asia is the only region with two nuclear-armed rivals in active territorial dispute. F_SAS events carry nuclear escalation risk that events in other regions do not.

## Calibration Notes

South Asian stress is elevated and rising. Climate vulnerability is increasing, water stress worsening, Pakistan fiscal situation fragile. Baseline probabilities reflect deteriorating trajectory.
