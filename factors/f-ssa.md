# F_SSA: Sub-Saharan Africa Crisis Factor

## Overview

The Sub-Saharan Africa Crisis factor represents regional pressure across Africa south of the Sahara—climate stress, demographic pressure, state fragility, and food insecurity. When F_SSA has a high realization, African humanitarian crises and state failures become more likely.

## What This Factor Captures

- Sahel desertification and conflict
- State fragility across multiple countries
- Demographic explosion (highest fertility region)
- Food insecurity and famine risk
- Climate vulnerability (drought, heat)
- Governance challenges
- Resource conflicts

## Events That Load Heavily on F_SSA

| Event | Loading | Rationale |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Sahel Catastrophe | 0.85 | Core regional event |
| Nigeria State Failure | 0.75 | Largest population |
| Ethiopian Fragmentation | 0.70 | Major regional power |
| DRC Further Collapse | 0.65 | Ongoing crisis |
| South African Collapse | 0.70 | Regional economic anchor |
| East African Famine | 0.75 | Recurrent crisis |

## Geographic Scope

F_SSA affects:
- Sahel belt (Mali, Niger, Burkina Faso, Chad)
- West Africa (Nigeria, Ghana, Côte d'Ivoire)
- East Africa (Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, Somalia)
- Central Africa (DRC, CAR, Cameroon)
- Southern Africa (South Africa, Zimbabwe, Mozambique)

The region contains ~1.2 billion people, rising to potentially 2-3 billion by 2100.

## Relationship to Other Factors

- **F_CLIM**: Very strong relationship. Africa is extremely climate-vulnerable.
- **F_FOOD**: Very strong relationship. Food insecurity is endemic.
- **F_HLTH**: Strong relationship. Health system weakness throughout.
- **F_EUR**: Moderate relationship via migration pressure.
- **F_FIN, F_GPT**: Weak relationship. Africa is peripheral to global financial/great power dynamics.

## "Trapped Population" Dynamic

Unlike other regions, much of Sub-Saharan Africa faces geographic barriers to migration (Sahara, Mediterranean). Populations facing the worst climate stress may be unable to reach potential refuge. This produces humanitarian catastrophe rather than migration crisis.

## Calibration Notes

Sub-Saharan Africa faces the most severe humanitarian risks of the 21st century. Multiple countries in the "potential catastrophe" tier in regional vulnerability assessments. Baseline probabilities reflect very high structural vulnerability.
