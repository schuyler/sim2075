# Digest: "Oil Price Fiction" and "P1 TACO Clock" wiki pages

*Research subagent report, July 10, 2026. Sources: Third Gulf War research wiki pages `Trends/Oil Price Fiction` (1,031 lines, confidence: high, updated 2026-06-30) and `Variables/P1 Taco Clock` (1,377 lines, confidence: medium, updated 2026-06-03). All quotes verbatim.*

**Framing notes:** (1) The TACO page never expands the acronym and carries confidence "medium"; the "confirmed" scoring lives on the linked `Hypothesis/Power Plant Taco` page. (2) The Day-40 ceasefire (announced at the 8pm April 7 deadline; wiki-dated Day 40, April 8) did **not** end the war — it fractured within 18 hours; the war ran on through a blockade and 8+ "inside-ceasefire" kinetic exchanges, ending with the Islamabad MOU around Day 110 (June 16–17). Day 40 confirmed the *behavioral hypothesis* (Trump backs down at the execution threshold), not the war's termination.

## 1. Oil Price Fiction

### Phase-by-phase

Seven-plus distinct "fiction" cycles — episodes where the reported price of oil decoupled from the physical cost of oil — plus a terminal regime change:

| Phase | Days | Signal | Physical reality | Gap | Resolution |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 9–11 | Trump rhetoric / IEA 400M bbl release / fabricated Navy-escort claim | Strait 97% closed, ~15M bpd stranded | ~$20–40 | Days — collapsed on Basra tanker attacks |
| 2 | 13–31 | Benchmarks vs. refined products | Crack spread blowout (refining capacity destroyed) | Structural | Persistent |
| 3 | 23–27 | Ceasefire expectations in futures | $32 spot-futures gap | $32, Gulf | Persistent |
| 4 | 40–41 | Presidential ceasefire announcement | Zero tankers transited; Saudi bypass pipeline struck | ~$20 crash/bounce | <24 hours |
| 5 | 43 | Islamabad talks | Forties Blend $147 ATH; Dated Brent $144+ | $50 — **global** | Pending |
| 6 | 46–52 | Blockade-as-containment | Dual blockade, zero traffic | ~$33–50 | Broke Day 54+ |
| 7 | 54–63+ | Escalation risk + demand destruction | Both sides converging | Narrowing from both ends | "Unstable equilibrium" |

**Phase 1 (founding episode):** "From March 9 to the evening of March 11, crude benchmarks were substantially disconnected from the physical supply picture. Brent traded in the high $80s while the underlying supply deficit — approaching double digits in millions of barrels per day — supported prices well above $100." Suppression ran through escalating "signal interventions": Trump's "pretty well complete" remark (−$30 intraday), the IEA's record 400M-barrel release proposal (−11%), and Energy Secretary Wright's *fabricated* claim that the Navy had escorted a tanker through Hormuz (−17% intraday, reversed within hours). It collapsed permanently when the Basra tanker attacks "overwhelmed the IEA's record 400M barrel reserve release" — Brent went ~$88→~$100 overnight.

**The core mechanism — the "definitional error":**

> "The physical price of oil — what a refiner actually pays for a barrel delivered today — is the price of oil. That is what 'price' means. On Day 43, that cost was $147 for Forties Blend. On Day 47, WTI futures traded at $91. These are not competing measurements. The futures contract is a bet on what oil will cost later, filtered through sentiment, hedging flows, and political signals carrying no physical supply information. It is often a useful bet. It is not the price of oil."

> "a political signal moves the derivative, the derivative is reported as 'the price,' and the public concludes that energy has gotten cheaper. It hasn't. The barrel still costs what the barrel costs."

**Signal credibility as a depletable resource:**

> "Signal interventions operate on borrowed credibility. Each intervention that does not correspond to a physical change in supply depletes the credibility stock available for the next one. Trump's 'ending soon' works once; the second time, the drop is smaller; by the fifth time, the market adjusts within minutes... Fabricated claims don't just fail — they actively destroy credibility, making the *next* true statement less credible."

The page explicitly notes this is "the same depletion logic that governs interceptor stocks and petroleum reserves: a finite buffer that buys time but cannot substitute for the underlying problem resolving."

### Mechanism inventory sustaining the fiction

1. **Rhetoric/announcement signals** — with a documented institutional channel: "they literally leak it to the same dude at Axios every week and it gets printed on and reported with the same slack-jawed credulity every week" (Devereaux, Day 90). The wiki: "it identifies the *reporting infrastructure* as a necessary condition for the Fiction's operation."
2. **Institutional interventions** — IEA/SPR releases ("Markets priced the announcement, not the delivery").
3. **Fabrication** — the Wright escort claim.
4. **"Infrastructure fiction"** — the OFAC Iranian-oil waiver: "a *real* policy action that cannot be *executed* because the underlying trade infrastructure doesn't exist" (no payment channels, no SWIFT, no insurance; Iranian oil offered to India *at a premium to Brent*).
5. **Reporting-structure artifact (Phase 2)** — media reports WTI/Brent, not crack spreads: "By the measures that actually determine what Americans pay — refined product prices up 85–120%, diesel at $185/bbl wholesale, crack spreads at near-record $50/bbl — the cost is roughly double what the headline number suggests." "Not a coordinated deception... a structural artifact of how oil markets are reported."
6. **Organized selling — the "$115 ceiling"** (Day 81): "(1) **Sovereign intervention.** Japan publicly acknowledged preparing to short crude futures using $1.4 trillion in FX reserves... (2) **Insider announcement shorts.** The Phase 4 ($950M) and Phase 5 ($920M) shorts both triggered from the $110 zone... (3) **Producer hedging.**" Plus possible front-running: CNBC documented volume spikes in e-Minis and oil futures "at approximately 6:50 AM ET — fifteen minutes before Trump's 7:05 AM Truth Social post."
7. **China's "invisible hand"** (Day 71, via Blas) — physical-side suppression: import cuts of ~3.5M bpd, cargo reselling, coal-to-chemicals substitution; physical premiums collapsed from +$30 to +$1.
8. **Buffer absorption** (Day 87, Brookings/Rapier) — "Without the buffers absorbing, no amount of organized selling could suppress the price... **buffer exhaustion is a calendar event, not a sentiment event**" (exhaustion estimated mid-July).

### What the surprise actually was — decomposition

**(a) Mechanism right, called in real time (not a surprise to the author):** Phase 1 correction predicted Day 11, confirmed Day 12 ("The hypothesis was established in the morning and confirmed the same evening"). Phase 4: prediction that a ceasefire announcement wouldn't reopen Hormuz confirmed in <24 hours (NYT/Kpler: zero tankers), falsifying Robin Brooks's convergence thesis.

**(b) Parameter failure — demand elasticity systematically over-modeled.** Day 91: "the demand side of the severity curve was over-modeled (substitution and elasticity repeatedly beating forecasts), even as the supply-side physical damage tracks as expected... both have so far resolved toward 'less acute than modeled, later than modeled.'" IEA's "~6 weeks of jet fuel left" warning "effectively been falsified." Establishment models had the mirror-image problem: Goldman "needed four revisions and 81 days to arrive at the supply picture the wiki described on Day 6."

**(c) Genuine mechanism failure — Phase 7 convergence.** The page's most direct self-criticism:

> "The convergence was not predicted by the wiki's framework. The Oil Price Fiction thesis has consistently predicted *upward* resolution — physical forcing futures higher through discontinuous repricing events. What happened instead was mutual convergence: the fiction dissolved not through a snap but through a squeeze from both ends. The supply shock raised the floor; the demand destruction lowered the ceiling."

The $35–50 gap (Day 43) compressed to ~$7 by Day 59 — a "stagflation pricing equilibrium... unstable." The fiction later ran **in reverse** (Day 89): "markets suppressed prices *below geopolitical reality*." By Day 92: a ballistic missile hit an American airbase in Kuwait, injured Americans, destroyed two Reapers — "Brent closed at ~$91.12, did not spike." "The deal-leak cycle's signal force now exceeds the market force of a ballistic missile hitting an American airbase."

**(d) Mechanisms nobody had in the model:** sovereign FX-defense shorting of crude (Japan); insider announcement shorts / possible presidential-post front-running; the institutionalized Axios deal-leak channel as market infrastructure; JPMorgan's finding that the mispricing "migrated from the futures-physical crude gap... to the product-crude crack... The fiction didn't resolve — it moved down the value chain."

**Framing correction the page adopts (Blas, Day 50):** "the financial and physical markets are not disconnected, and the financial market is not broken. They are doing different jobs. The physical market prices today's scarcity (next 30 days). The financial market prices a probability-weighted distribution of outcomes two months to ten years out... The error... is in the reporting convention that treats the forward-looking number as a description of the present." Bouchouev's asymmetry: physical market lost 10M barrels; financial market traded "an extra billion paper barrels." Blas's calibration for the "real" price: **~$125** vs. WTI's $83.85 — and a 3.7x geographic spread ($78 Kansas → $286 delivered Sri Lanka; insurance from 25bp to 5%, no war coverage).

**Endgame:** After the MOU (Day 110), the fiction ran in the de-escalation direction — gas fell 7 straight weeks to $3.78 while "the SPR hit a new multi-decade low (lowest since May 1983)," transits stayed thin, the main channel stayed mined. "Honest read: relief is real for consumers today and structurally borrowed against an unrecovered physical system." Moody's realized cost: ~$1,000 per US household.

## 2. The TACO Clock (P1)

### Definition and the three-clock model

P1 is "one of four hidden parameters tracked daily. P1 monitors the dominant variable determining when and how the war ends: Trump's political and psychological tolerance for continuing the conflict." Three-clock model: Clock One (Iran's kinetic capacity), Clock Two (allied interceptor stocks/P2), Clock Three (Trump's tolerance — "Runs on gas prices, poll numbers, cost per day ($11.3B for first six days), the diplomatic calendar, energy executive pressure, and the attention span of a president who likes to win").

> "**The TACO Clock is dominant.** The kinetic clocks constrain what is *possible*. But the TACO Clock constrains what *happens*. The war ends when Trump says it ends, not when the missiles run out."

Foundational evidence (Day 14, Kilmeade Show), Trump on when he'd know the war is over: **"When I feel it. When I feel it in my bones."** The wiki: "the single most important P1 statement of the war. It confirms — in Trump's own words... that war termination is governed by one person's subjective psychology, not military objectives, intelligence assessments, or casualty thresholds." WSJ Day-14 reporting: Gen. Caine warned pre-war that attack could close Hormuz; Trump "acknowledged the risk but moved forward, telling his team that Tehran would 'likely capitulate before closing the strait'"; CIA warned killing Khamenei could produce hardliner succession (it did — Mojtaba); planning circle was Vance/Rubio/Hegseth only. "One person's psychology governs the war, filtered through an inner circle that was selected precisely because it wouldn't push back."

### Daily tracking

~40 preserved daily assessments (Day 14 → Day 96), each ending in a "Net P1 assessment" and a running scenario allocation (B1 = deal/resolution, B2 = escalation/resumption, B3 = managed-crisis equilibrium; e.g., Day 96: "Hold B2 55% / B1 20% / B3 25%"). Key indicators: Trump rhetoric pattern ("the oscillation itself is data"), advisor visibility, congressional pressure (WPR vote trajectory 219→214→212→212-212→215-208 passage), energy-industry pressure, polling, diesel and gasoline prices ("the consumer-facing P1 input"), China-summit status, Polymarket, CENTCOM's planning horizon. Key uncertainties end with: "**Trump's private psychological state.** A 79-year-old man waging a major war; no structural model fully predicts this."

### Threshold behavior and compression

- **The OPG ("Operational Pause-and-Go") pattern:** an escalating ladder of deadlines and stand-downs — "March 13 'feel it in my bones,' May 1 binary framing, May 5 '2-3 more weeks,' May 7 'love tap,' May 11 'one week,' Day 77 'to be continued'..." — reaching "**eight stand-down cycles to date, zero conversions to sustained kinetic action**." Day 81 (Axios): Trump hadn't actually decided to strike before announcing the "pause" — "The decision apparatus exists as a *performance of deliberation*, not as a mechanism that produces kinetic outputs." Bridges *were* struck (Day 36 — first rung executed); power plants — the named next rung, with the "Tuesday, 8:00 P.M." ultimatum — were not.
- **Day 40 confirmation:** Trump's Easter-weekend ultimatum (Day 37) set power-plant strikes for Tuesday 8pm ET; at that exact threshold, "Trump announced a 2-week ceasefire at 8pm on April 7." At the moment of maximum committed threat, he chose ceasefire.
- **Compression:** the oscillation period shortened over the war — Day 69 "love tap" ("the TACO Clock's most compressed cycle yet"); Day 89: "soften (Day 87 HEU opening) → harden (Day 89 Camp David) → soften within the same statement... **The oscillation cycle compressed from days to sentences.**" The same compression metric governs the oil fiction's half-life (days → overnight → <24h → ~60h weekend cycle → same-session).

### Off-ramps analyzed (inventory and systematic closure)

1. **The June 2025 (Twelve-Day War) mechanism cannot repeat** — five structural reasons: no Khamenei to order IRGC stand-down (Mojtaba lacks authority; IRGC runs "pre-delegated mosaic defense"); the US is co-belligerent, not mediator; Israeli coalition investment; damage too severe for mutual victory claims; conflict metastasized beyond the bilateral.
2. **Gulf/Qatar mediation** — progressively destroyed (Ras Laffan strike; Qatar "attacked by every major actor"), though the channel later demonstrated real function (Day 80: Qatar's Emir, MBS, MBZ jointly talked Trump out of a named, dated strike).
3. **Counterparty elimination** — decapitation shrank the negotiating pool; Pakistan had Araghchi/Qalibaf removed from Israel's hit list: "if they are also eliminated then there is no one else to talk to."
4. **The Xi summit** — tracked for months as the exit forcing function; formally closed Day 79.
5. **Unilateral victory declaration ("the fake off-ramp")** — Day-12 prediction, later validated from inside: the IC studied it and "found both variants fail: withdraw → Iran wins; stay → Iran sees it as a tactic" (Day 60); The Atlantic (Day 70): Trump "really wants the war to end" and is "convinced that he can sell any sort of agreement as a win."
6. **The deal/MOU track** — which eventually worked (Day 110), through the structure the wiki described: declare whatever's available a win.

### Predictive or descriptive?

**Genuinely predictive at the structural level, and early:**
- The dominance thesis (Day 7) — externally validated repeatedly: Axios Day 54 ("the president thinks the US has achieved everything it can militarily and wants out"); Israeli intelligence Day 53 (Trump's statements "may not be based on a concrete plan, but reflect shifting moods and negotiating tactics").
- The "fake off-ramp" prediction (Day 12) → materialized as "Epic Fury is over" (Day 67) and the sell-any-deal-as-win exit.
- Timing convergence: Day 25 identification of April 9 as the war-termination target window ("Passover Ceasefire" hypothesis) landed within 48 hours of the actual Day 40 (April 8) ceasefire — via "a wildly nonlinear path."
- The OPG base rate became a working predictive tool: by Day 84 the framework explicitly bet "The OPG pattern predicts cycle 9" against the strongest kinetic-signal constellation of the war — and was right.
- Method lesson captured (Passover retrospective / "pessimistic anchor bias"): "when the TACO Clock (Trump's psychology) is pointing toward exit, we systematically underestimate how long that condition holds, because we overweight subordinate-variable evidence against the dominant variable." Their one big miss (Day 36 downgrade) came from overriding their own dominant variable.

**Not predictive at the timing/binary level, admitted:**
- Scenario probabilities oscillated without converting: Day 63's B2 jump to 38% ("the largest single-day increase in wiki history") produced no strikes; Day 96: "every prior forcing function... resolved into continued drift." Day 89: "**The oscillation pattern is the assessment.**"
- `Hypothesis/Ground Campaign Launch` "scored as a failure and downgraded" — "The ceasefire was the intervening variable the prediction didn't model."
- Individual stand-downs rated ~50/50 in real time.

**Fair summary:** predictive about the class of outcome and the shape of the endgame (exit via face-saving declaration; threats that don't convert; ceasefire ≠ Hormuz reopening); descriptive-only about which day any given threat would fire. Its best predictive engine wasn't psychology-modeling but an accumulated **base rate of the actor's own behavior** (8–9 cycles, zero conversions).

## 3. Bearing on conditional-on-decision structural forecasting

### Where conditional/mechanistic reasoning clearly worked
- **Physical supply-side logistics tracked as modeled**: "the supply-side physical damage tracks as expected." Multi-wave thesis (Day 6), refining-bottleneck analysis ("the 2005 Katrina lesson... on a global scale"), mine-clearance timelines, insurance dynamics, escort-coalition failure — all held; the establishment (Goldman, IEA, Rystad, oil-major CEOs) arrived at the same numbers 40–80 days later.
- **Explicitly conditional predictions performed well**: "A ceasefire itself would do little or nothing to impact oil prices directly, unless it directly and clearly impacts the... Strait of Hormuz" — confirmed in <24 hours. Brookings' buffer-exhaustion model was explicitly two-branched, and the MOU branch fired.
- **Depletion-stock reasoning generalized across domains**: signal credibility, interceptor stocks, SPR, and floating-storage buffers all obeyed the same "finite buffer buys time" mechanism; the wiki used one to predict the others.

### Where it failed — decomposition
- **Parameter failure**: demand elasticity/substitution "repeatedly beating forecasts" (the Shallower Curve). Right mechanism, wrong magnitudes.
- **Mechanism failure**: Phase 7 mutual convergence ("not predicted by the wiki's framework") and the bidirectional fiction (Day 89). The model had a one-directional error term and lacked the demand-side squeeze and organized-selling ceiling.
- **Novel mechanisms**: sovereign FX-motivated crude shorting; insider announcement shorts / possible front-running; the institutionalized leak channel; "infrastructure fiction" (legal permission without executable trade plumbing).

### The most important cross-cutting finding

**Oil *prices* turned out not to be a clean structural channel — they were contaminated by the Small-N actor variable.** The oil page directly: Phase 6's durability "depends on the TACO Clock more than the physical supply fundamentals — **because the market is pricing Trump's psychology, not barrels.**" The Oil Price Fiction is, in large part, the TACO Clock projected into asset prices. Decomposition of the war's "real surprise": physical logistics = tractable and correctly forecast; price formation = a hybrid channel where leader psychology, reflexive media infrastructure, and strategic sellers leak in. **Refinement for the sim2075 framework: "structural consequences conditional on decisions" holds for *physical* transmission (barrels, refineries, mines, inventories, displacement flows) much better than for *market* transmission, because markets are themselves forecasting the intractable actor.**

Supporting observations: **Polymarket beat the oil market at pricing the pattern** (Day 82: oil crashed 6% on "final stages" while prediction markets put permanent peace by May 31 at 17% — "oil trades on the rhetoric, prediction markets trade on the pattern"); Devereaux's twin formulations — "war does not obey the logic of markets" and "**Trump can't TACO unilaterally, he needs Iran to let him**" — the latter flagging that even the single-leader variable is interactive: Iran's institutional structure (IRGC autonomy, Mojtaba's weakness, the Sacred Obligation Override) blocked exits Trump wanted.

The wiki's own meta-lesson (Meta/Analytical Principles, via the Passover retrospective) is the cleanest statement of how to use an intractable dominant variable without pretending to predict it: identify which variable is dominant, hold that identification against noisier subordinate evidence, and expect the dominant variable to reach the structurally predicted endpoint "through a wildly nonlinear path" — while refusing to call the path's individual steps.
